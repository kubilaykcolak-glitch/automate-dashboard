# UK Self Assessment — key dates and penalty rules

The UK tax year runs **6 April to 5 April**. Self Assessment (SA) covers sole traders, partners, landlords, and anyone else with income outside PAYE.

## Critical dates for any given tax year ending 5 April YYYY

| Date | Deadline |
|---|---|
| 5 October YYYY | Register for SA if newly self-employed during the tax year just ended. Miss this and there is a "failure to notify" penalty regardless of whether tax is due. |
| 31 October YYYY | Paper return filing deadline. |
| 30 December YYYY | File online by this date if you want HMRC to collect tax owed (up to £3,000) via your PAYE tax code the following year. |
| 31 January YYYY+1 | **Online filing deadline** AND balancing payment for the tax year AND first payment on account for the next tax year. The big one. |
| 31 July YYYY+1 | Second payment on account for the current tax year. |

So for the tax year ending 5 April 2025:
- Register by **5 October 2025**.
- Online return + balancing payment + first POA: **31 January 2026**.
- Second POA: **31 July 2026**.

## Payments on account (POA)

If the previous tax year's bill was more than £1,000 **and** less than 80% of tax was collected at source (e.g. PAYE), HMRC requires two payments on account toward the current year's bill. Each POA equals **half of last year's tax bill**.

Example. 2024/25 tax bill = £6,000.
- 31 Jan 2026: £6,000 balancing payment + £3,000 first POA for 2025/26 = £9,000.
- 31 Jul 2026: £3,000 second POA for 2025/26.
- 31 Jan 2027: 2025/26 balancing payment (positive or negative) + £X first POA for 2026/27.

The user can apply to **reduce** payments on account (form SA303 or online) if they expect this year's profits to be lower. Reducing them incorrectly attracts interest if the actual liability turns out to be higher.

## Late-filing penalties

| How late | Penalty |
|---|---|
| 1 day late | £100 (fixed, even if no tax due) |
| 3 months late | + £10/day for up to 90 days (max £900) |
| 6 months late | + £300 or 5% of tax due, whichever is higher |
| 12 months late | + another £300 or 5% of tax due |

Worst case: a return 12 months late with no tax owed still costs £1,600 in penalties.

## Late-payment penalties (separate from filing)

| How late | Penalty |
|---|---|
| 30 days late | 5% of unpaid tax |
| 6 months late | + another 5% |
| 12 months late | + another 5% |

**Plus** interest at HMRC's official rate from the original due date until paid. Interest rates change with Bank of England base rate — check current rate at gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals before quoting.

## Reasonable excuse appeals

HMRC may cancel penalties if the user has a "reasonable excuse" — e.g. serious illness, bereavement, postal disruption, HMRC's own systems failing. Software not working, forgetting, or finding the system too difficult are NOT reasonable excuses.

If a user reports a missed deadline:
1. File and pay immediately to stop further penalties.
2. Ask whether they have any genuinely exceptional circumstance.
3. If so, point them to the SA370 form to appeal.

## Making Tax Digital for Income Tax (MTD ITSA)

From **April 2026**, sole traders and landlords with combined business + property income over £50,000 must:
- Keep digital records.
- Submit quarterly updates to HMRC via MTD-compatible software.
- Submit an end-of-period statement and final declaration annually.

Threshold drops to £30,000 from April 2027. Always check the latest gov.uk page before quoting these as final — the rollout has been delayed multiple times.

## What to ask the user

When a user mentions Self Assessment, before answering:
- Which tax year are they asking about? (Get explicit dates, not "this year".)
- Are they registered with HMRC for SA already?
- Do they have payments on account on file?
- Have they filed in time?

These four answers turn a vague question into a precise one.
